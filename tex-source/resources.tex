\metatitle{Resources}
\metadescription{Useful links, suggested books and software resources related to symmetric functions and algebraic combinatorics.}


\section[resources]{More resources}


For an introduction to the topic of symmetric functions and algebraic combinatorics,
I recommend the following books. 
\name{Richard Stanley}, \emph{Enumerative Combinatorics II} \cite{StanleyEC2}, 
\name{Lynne Butler}, \emph{Subgroup Lattices and Symmetric Functions} \cite{Butler1994}, 
\name{Bruce Sagan}, \emph{The Symmetric Group} \cite{Sagan2001}, 
\name{William Fulton}, \emph{Young Tableaux} \cite{Fulton1997} and 
\name{Ian Macdonald}, \emph{Symmetric functions and Hall polynomials} \cite{Macdonald1995}.

Moreover, \name{David Bressoud} --- \emph{Proofs and confirmations: the story of 
the alternating sign matrix conjecture} \cite{Bressoud1999}, is a very nice reading.
\name{Angèle M. Foley}, \name{Alejandro H. Morales}, \name{Amarpreet Rattan},
and \name{Karen Yeats} survey the work of \name{Ian P. Goulden} and
\name{David M. Jackson}, including their contributions to classical
enumeration, symmetric functions, permutation factorizations, and algebraic
structures in quantum field theory \cite{FoleyMoralesRattanYeats2022}.
\name{Igor Pak} gives a broad survey of what it means for a formula to have a
combinatorial interpretation, with many examples, open problems, and a
complexity-theoretic perspective \cite{Pak2024}.

\subsection[softwareLinks]{Software resources}

\begin{itemize}
\item \icon{database} \href{https://oeis.org/}{OEIS} --- database of integer sequences.

\item \icon{database} \href{https://www.findstat.org/}{Findstat} --- database and search engine for combinatorial statistics and maps.

\item \icon{database} \href{https://math.depaul.edu/~bridget/patterns.html}{permutation pattern database} by \name{Bridget Tenner}.

\item \icon{github} \href{https://github.com/PerAlexandersson/Mathematica-packages}{Mathematica packages} ---
Packages for Catalan objects, symmetric functions, permutation statistics,
tableaux, \(q\)-analogs, and related computations.

\item \icon{github} \href{https://github.com/PerAlexandersson/polynomial-tools}{polynomial-tools} ---
Exact univariate polynomial tools for
\hyperref[realRootedPolynomial]{real-rootedness},
\hyperref[interlacingPolynomials]{interlacing}, and combinatorial experiments.

\item \icon{github} \href{https://github.com/PerAlexandersson/RealRooted}{RealRooted} ---
An experimental Lean 4 library for
\hyperref[realRootedPolynomial]{real-rooted polynomials},
\hyperref[interlacingPolynomials]{interlacing}, compatibility, and
Pólya-frequency arguments.

\item \icon{github} \href{https://github.com/PerAlexandersson/kostka}{kostka} ---
Rust code for \hyperref[kostkaCoefficient]{Kostka coefficients},
\hyperref[ehrhartPolynomialDefinition]{Ehrhart polynomials} of
\hyperref[gtPolytope]{Gelfand--Tsetlin polytopes}, and
\hyperref[hStarVector]{\(h^*\)-vectors}.

\item \icon{github} \href{https://github.com/PerAlexandersson/lrcalc-rs}{lrcalc-rs} ---
A Rust implementation with a liblrcalc-compatible interface for
Littlewood--Richardson coefficient computations.

\item \icon{github} \href{https://github.com/PerAlexandersson/arxiv.symmetricfunctions.com}{arxiv.symmetricfunctions.com} ---
Source code for the arXiv companion, including combinatorics browsing,
DOI discovery, and BibTeX helper tools.
\end{itemize}
