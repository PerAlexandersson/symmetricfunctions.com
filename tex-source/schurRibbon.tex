The \defin{ribbon Schur polynomials} are
the \hyperref[schurSkew]{skew Schur polynomials}
indexed by \hyperref[skewShapes]{ribbons}.
In particular, Gessel's fundamental expansion for skew Schur functions gives
\[
  \schurS_\alpha(\xvec)
  =
  \sum_{T \in \SYT(\alpha)} \gessel_{D(T)}(\xvec),
\]
where $\alpha$ is a ribbon and $D(T)$ is the descent set of $T$.

There are $2^{n-1}$ different ribbons $\alpha$ with $n$ boxes,
but the corresponding ribbon Schur functions $\schurS_\alpha$
are distinct, see \cite{Rubey2010x}.

\name{Amanda Riehl} uses ribbon Schur identities to compute generating
functions for permutations with prescribed descent sets \cite{Riehl2008}.
She also studies the dual basis to the ribbon Schur functions $Z_\lambda$
indexed by partitions, giving a combinatorial interpretation for the
monomial coefficients of the dual basis and applications to expansions
between arbitrary ribbon Schur functions and those indexed by partitions.

\name{Peter McNamara} and \name{Stephanie van Willigenburg} study the
\hyperref[schurPositivity]{Schur-positivity} order on
\hyperref[schurSkew]{skew Schur functions}, with particular attention to
ribbons \cite{McNamaraWilligenburg2009Ribbon}.
For ribbons with a fixed
number of rows, they show that the corresponding subposet is convex.  They
also give a complete description for multiplicity-free ribbons: such a
ribbon has at most two rows and at most two columns of length greater than
one, and the resulting Schur-positivity poset is essentially a product of
two chains.

\subsection[schurRibbonIntoH]{Complete homogeneous expansion}

The ribbon Schur function $\schurS_\alpha$ can be expanded in the
\hyperref[completeH]{complete homogeneous basis} as
\[
   \sum_{\beta \geq \alpha} (-1)^{\length(\beta)-\length(\alpha)}\completeH_{\beta}
\]
where the sum is over coarsenings of $\alpha$, see \cite{Moustakas2023x}.
A generalization to \emph{colored ribbon Schur functions} can also be found in that reference.

\subsection[schurRibbonProduct]{Products of ribbon Schur functions}

For \hyperref[compositions]{compositions} $\alpha=(\alpha_1,\dotsc,\alpha_k)$ and
$\beta=(\beta_1,\dotsc,\beta_\ell)$, let
$\alpha\cdot\beta$ denote \defin{concatenation} and let
\[
  \alpha\odot\beta
  \coloneqq (\alpha_1,\dotsc,\alpha_{k-1},\alpha_k+\beta_1,\beta_2,\dotsc,\beta_\ell)
\]
denote \defin{near concatenation}.  Then ribbon Schur functions satisfy
\[
  \schurS_\alpha\schurS_\beta
  =
  \schurS_{\alpha\cdot\beta}+\schurS_{\alpha\odot\beta},
\]
see \cite{BilleraThomasWilligenburg2006}.  In that paper, these
multiplicative relations are also used to describe the algebra generated by
the ribbon Schur functions and to classify when two ribbon Schur functions are
equal.

Their complete homogeneous expansions are
\[
  \schurS_{(1,1)}=\completeH_1^2-\completeH_2
  \qquad\text{and}\qquad
  \schurS_{(2)}=\completeH_2.
\]
The product rule gives, for instance,
\[
  \schurS_{(1,1)}\schurS_{(2)}
  =
  \schurS_{(1,1,2)}+\schurS_{(1,3)}.
\]
\end{example}
