The family of polynomials referred to as \emph{generalized Demazure atoms}
were introduced in \cite{HaglundMasonRemmel2012} and further studied in \cite{RemmelTiefenbruck2013}.

The generalized Demazure atoms are related to the fillings in the combinatorial model for
\hyperref[macdonaldE]{non-symmetric Macdonald polynomials}, \cite{HaglundHaimanLoehr2008} and
\hyperref[macdonaldEperm]{permuted-basement Macdonald polynomials}, \cite{Alexandersson2015gbMacdonald},
but the definitions differ.
The families studied in \cite{Alexandersson2015gbMacdonald} and \cite{AlexanderssonSawhney2019}
are referred to as \emph{permuted-basement atoms} and are not the same as 
generalized Demazure atoms.

The generalized Demazure atoms have nice properties with respect to insertion algorithms and \hyperref[rsk]{RSK},
while the permuted-basement atoms are compatible with Demazure--Lusztig operators and representation theory.
For a fixed basement $\sigma$, Haglund--Mason--Remmel's generalized
Demazure atoms $\widehat{E}^{\sigma}_\gamma$ refine Schur functions:
\[
\schurS_\beta(\xvec)=\sum_{\lambda(\gamma)=\beta}
  \widehat{E}^{\sigma}_\gamma(\xvec).
\]
Here, $\lambda(\gamma)$ is the decreasing rearrangement of $\gamma$.
For the reverse-identity basement this sum has a single nonzero term, so
Schur functions occur as a subfamily.  For the identity basement one recovers
Mason's Demazure atoms.  The $t$-deformed basement families are recorded here
as \hyperref[permutedBasementTAtom]{permuted-basement $t$-atoms}.

\name{Janine LoBue Tiefenbruck} and \name{Jeffrey B. Remmel} prove a
Murnaghan--Nakayama rule for generalized Demazure atoms
\cite{RemmelTiefenbruck2013}.  Their rule expands the product of a
power-sum symmetric function with a generalized Demazure atom as a signed
sum of generalized Demazure atoms.
