\section[fussCatalanObjects]{Generalizations of Catalan numbers}

The \defin{$k$-Catalan numbers} or \defin{Fuß--Catalan numbers} are defined as
\[
  \catalan^k(n) \coloneqq \frac{1}{kn+1}\binom{kn+1}{n}.
\]
There are many interpretations of these,
see, for example, \cite{StanleyCatalan}.



The \defin{s-binomial coefficients} are defined via 
\[
  (1+x+x^2+\dotsb+x^s)^n = \sum_{j=0}^{sn} \binom{n}{j}_s x^j.
\]
The \defin{s-Catalan numbers} are then defined as 
\[
\catalan^s(n) \coloneqq \binom{2n}{sn}_s  - \binom{2n}{sn}_{s+1}.
\]
There is a relationship between s-Catalan numbers
and \hyperref[littlewoodRichardsonModels]{Littlewood--Richardson coefficients},
see \cite{Linz2021x}.
In fact, let $\delta = (n,n-1,\dotsc,2,1)$.
Then
\[
c^{2 \delta_{2n}}_{2 \delta_{2n-1} , (sn,sn)} = \binom{2n}{sn}_s  - \binom{2n}{sn}_{s+1}.
\]
Note that the left hand side is a polynomial in $s$, due to 
a result by E.~Rassart \cite{Rassart2004}.

\name{François Bergeron} and \name{Mikhail Mazin} introduce triangular
partitions, whose cells lie below a line segment from $(r,0)$ to $(0,s)$
\cite{BergeronMazin2022x}.  Partitions contained in a triangular partition
generalize Dyck paths and \hyperref[parkingFunctions]{parking functions}, and
the induced subposet of Young lattice has a planar Hasse diagram with a
generalized first-return recurrence.

\name{Volodymyr Mazorchuk} studies the sequence \oeis{A393920}, counting
extension-closed additive idempotent-split subcategories for uniformly
oriented type $A$ quivers \cite{Mazorchuk2026x}.  The paper gives a
recurrence using subsets of triangular arrays of lattice points, relates a
quotient restriction graph to Fibonacci numbers, identifies a Catalan sequence
on the boundary of the recurrence, and shows that a companion sequence counts
convex topologies on finite chains.


\section[setPartitions]{Set partitions}

\name{Amritanshu Prasad} and \name{Samrith Ram} introduce polynomials indexed
by integer partitions which interpolate between several familiar objects
\cite{PrasadRam2025}.  At $1$ they count set partitions of prescribed block
sizes, at $0$ they count standard tableaux of a fixed shape, and at $-1$ they
count standard shifted tableaux of that shape; the same polynomials also count
subspace profiles over finite fields at prime powers.


\section[skewSYT]{Skew standard Young tableaux}

For formulas for the number of skew SYT, see \cite{MoralesZhu2020x}.
\name{Igor Pak} gives a case-based introduction to asymptotic algebraic
combinatorics for skew shapes, centered on thick ribbons
\cite{Pak2021SkewShapeAsymptotics}.

\name{Darij Grinberg}, \name{Nazar Korniichuk},
\name{Kostiantyn Molokanov}, and \name{Severyn Khomych} give a new proof of a
unified generating-function identity combining the Pak--Postnikov and Naruse
skew hook-length formulas \cite{GrinbergKorniichukMolokanovKhomych2023x}.
The proof uses recurrences, determinants, and elementary combinatorics.
\name{Tatsushi Shimazaki} relates hook-length products for adjacent staircase
partitions to special values of Jacobi polynomials \cite{Shimazaki2026x}.
The same identities give special values of stable
\hyperref[grothendieck]{Grothendieck polynomials} and
\hyperref[schurKP]{$K$-theoretic Schur $P$-functions}, with coefficients
described by excited Young diagrams.

\section[setValuedTableaux]{Set-valued tableaux}

\name{Anders Buch} introduced set-valued tableaux in \cite{Buch2002}, 
in order to study the K-theoretical Grassmannian.

In \cite{Drube2018}, it is shown that $\catalan^k(n)$
is equal to the number of set-valued SYT of shape $(n,n)$,
where each box in the first row has one entry, while the boxes in the second row
all have exactly $k-1$ entries. Note that for $k=2$, we recover the classical 
Catalan numbers, which count the number of SYT of shape $(n,n)$.
Note that the total number of entries is $kn$.
