\metadescription{Definitions and background qt-Catalan numbers, qt-Kostka numbers and related qt-symmetries.}


\section[qtCatalan]{Introduction}

For an introduction to the topic of $qt$-Catalan numbers and $qt$-Kostka numbers,
see J. Haglund's book, \cite{qtCatalanBook} and F. Bergeron \cite{Bergeron2009}.



Let $DP(n)$ be the set of Dyck paths of size $n$. 
Then the $qt$-Catalan numbers can then be defined as
\[
\catalan_n(q,t) \coloneqq \sum_{P \in DP(n)} q^{\area(P)} t^{\bounce(P)}.
\]
These are symmetric in $(q,t)$, but a combinatorial proof of this fact is not known.

There are various other ways to define these polynomials, for example
\[
\catalan_n(q,t) = \sum_{P \in DP(n)} q^{\dinv(P)} t^{\area(P)},
\]
or via \hyperref[diagonalHarmonics]{diagonal harmonics} as $\langle DH_n(q,t), \elementaryE_{n} \rangle$,
see \cite[Thm. I.2]{GarsiaHaglund2000}.



\subsection[qtCatalanGeneralizations]{Generalizations}

In \cite{QuZhang2025x}, a $qt$-Catalan symmetry is proved involving 
the two statistics $(area,depth)$ as well as $(dinv,ddinv)$.
They also provide proofs in the $\vec{k}$-Dyck path setting.
